Industrial Pipeline
West Valley is the fastest-moving corridor in the metro
Permit filings in the Loop 303 / I-10 interchange zone are at their highest volume since 2005, driven by logistics and last-mile distribution demand. Owner groups are accelerating timelines — projects that would have been 2027 starts are moving to Q4 2026 across multiple active sites. GC shortlists are forming 4–6 weeks earlier than typical cycle.
Medical Office / MOB
Healthcare developer activity resurgent post-consolidation
Three major healthcare systems are expanding outpatient footprint across the Phoenix metro following 2024 consolidation activity. Medical office construction is at the highest pace since 2019. Owner groups are prioritizing GCs with healthcare experience and infection control protocols. Meridian's MOB experience is a competitive differentiator in this segment.
Labor Market
Tilt-up and structural concrete crews in short supply
Three large industrial projects started simultaneously in Q2 absorbed most available tilt-up concrete labor in the East Valley. GCs without self-perform concrete or established crew relationships are pricing a 6–8 week labor delay into bids. Meridian's self-perform capability is a pricing and schedule advantage that owners are actively prioritizing.
Competitive Landscape
Mid-tier GC field consolidating — fewer qualified bidders
Two established mid-tier GCs in the $10M–$40M range retired or merged in the past 18 months. Owner groups that relied on those firms are actively building new GC relationships. This is creating unusual openness to introductory conversations from GCs without prior relationship history — an environment Meridian should capitalize on this quarter.
